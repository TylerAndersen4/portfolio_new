About

Background

Before I was a designer I was an English teacher and (sometimes) writer. I worked abroad in Italy, Argentina, and France before moving to NYC to pursue a career in tech. I stopped in Los Angeles to realize my dream of having a creative profession.

My relationship with design began in the pages of Vogue. The first 12 pages - the ads - are always the most exciting. This is where all of the major brands display their latest campaigns for the season. I didn't know it at the time, but these were my first interactions with the power of graphic design. I was fascinated by the way that imagery and layout came together to tell a cohesive brand story.

Now as a professional designer, I can unpack these experiences and understand why fashion ads left such an imprint in my mind. By understanding the science behind layout and hierarchy, I see how elements come together. My work as a UX Designer is the intersection of my skills and interests: visual creativity, business acumen, and technical precision. Read on to learn about my design philosophy.

Design philosophy

1

Design is simple.

Simple design is the product of iteration, feedback, and testing. Through this process, the end product contains only the essential elements.

2

Design has intent.

Everything in design has a purpose. Successful design has a clear intention behind each element.

3

Design creates joy.

Good design releases endorphins - the user forms an emotional attachment to the product because of a pleasing experience.

Creating Products and Experiences

As a UX Designer, I create experiences that are enjoyable and backed in research. I believe that design should be driven by data and business solutions alongside the visual elements. Feel free to reach out if you have a project you'd like to begin collaborating on.
