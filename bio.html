“Who am I?” The first thought in my head: “I’m a mix of places, people, visuals and creativity.” It all comes together in my work.

People and Places

I’m fascinated by cultures, places, and languages. In high school I learned Spanish, and then in college, studied Italian and French. This fascination led me to study abroad for a year in Italy during school. After graduating, I moved to Buenos Aires to teach English and after that taught English at a public school outside of Paris. Before moving back to LA where I am now, I lived in Brooklyn while working for a tech startup in the Manhattan. While living in these different cities, I spent a lot of time traveling to nearby places and learning about different ways of life. I immersed myself by studying the local language and living my day-to-day life in it.

Visuals

Looking at the ads has always been my favorite part of reading Vogue. I ripped out and saved my favorite ones, and looking back now, can see how some of these images became the basis for my personal aesthetic. Aside from the ads, I spend time every season watching the runway shows from my favorite designers. The Avante-Garde work of designers like Miuccia Prada and Alexander Mcqueen completely reconfigured my understanding of what functional design is and can be.

Creativity

I started painting on 18 x 24” canvases that I would buy in bulk from the art store. As I grew up, I started doing larger pieces on plywood that my dad didn’t need anymore. His garage became my studio as I experimented with spraypainting Craigslist personals quotes across the surfaces. When I started traveling it wasn’t feasible to bring paint supplies, so I moved toward writing, photography, and digital collage. Aside from painting, last year I published two pieces of creative writing in an LA-based arts magazine, with a third piece coming this year in the same magazine. This latest piece will be accompanied by my friend’s paintings and is an exploration of artistic creation and gender identity.

So how does this all come together?

With my interest in people, languages, and visual design, I thought about a career in UX. I wanted to do something “creative” that also involved people, and after working at a startup I knew I wanted to stay in the tech industry. In the same way with fashion, I’m drawn to the high energy and constant iteration of tech. I believe that people and experiences come into our life when we need them, and UX appeared to me at a time when I needed direction and focus. So far it’s been a meaningful and challenging experience, and I’m excited about where it’s going.
